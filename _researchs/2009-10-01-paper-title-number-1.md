Return predictability varies across assets and over time rather than belonging solely to a particular predictor set or model. We develop a tree-based clustering framework that groups U.S. equities using rich firm characteristics together with market-wide predictors, making it possible to identify which assets are more predictable and under which market conditions. The strongest predictability appears among stocks with high earnings surprises, high earnings-to-price ratios, and low trading volume; it weakens when market dividend yield is low and becomes strongest when dividend yield is high and market liquidity is limited. Out of sample, the resulting predictability-differential strategy generates large monthly alphas, and portfolios formed from highly predictable clusters substantially outperform conventional benchmarks.
