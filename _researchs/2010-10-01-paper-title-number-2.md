We propose a deep-learning framework that directly estimates tangency-portfolio weights for large cross sections of individual assets. The method augments benchmark factors with a tradable long-short deep factor constructed from high-dimensional firm characteristics, allowing the deep factor to serve both as a market-hedging portfolio and as a way to span missing risks embedded in rich cross-sectional information. Applied to U.S. corporate bonds, the framework produces strong out-of-sample risk-adjusted performance and deep tangency portfolios that outperform standard observable and latent-factor benchmarks. The evidence also shows that corporate bond returns incorporate broad information from equity and option characteristics, pointing to a closer integration between bond and equity markets.
